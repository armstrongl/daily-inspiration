Chronic pain may not be lingering damage signals — it may be a brain decision. Researchers at CU Boulder identified a small circuit called the caudal granular insular cortex (CGIC) that acts as a gate: it determines whether acute pain fades normally or transitions into chronic pain. When they shut the circuit down in animal models, chronic pain didn't develop — and existing chronic pain stopped. The finding reframes pain not as a signal from the body but as a verdict from the brain. [Source](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260426012317.htm)

*Chosen because it fundamentally reframes chronic pain from a signal problem to a decision problem, which contradicts the default "pain = damage" schema.*

Chemist Grace Han's lab at UC Santa Barbara built a molecule that bottles the sun. The pyrimidone molecule, inspired by reversible structural changes in DNA, twists into a high-energy shape when hit by sunlight and holds that energy for years — then releases it as heat when triggered, enough to boil water. At 1.6 megajoules per kilogram, it stores roughly double the energy of a lithium-ion battery, all in a single recyclable molecule. Published in *Science*, February 2026. [Source](https://news.ucsb.edu/2026/022384/ucsb-scientists-bottle-sun-liquid-battery)

*Chosen because the concept of storing a star's energy in molecular bonds — and the bio-inspired elegance of the design — produces genuine conceptual vastness.*

Henrik Karlsson describes picking up Imre Lakatos's *Proofs and Refutations* — a book of mathematical philosophy by a Hungarian Stalinist — and realizing it was secretly about writing. Lakatos showed that proofs advance not by being right the first time, but by being put forward, attacked, and revised. Karlsson applies this to writing: the first draft isn't a demonstration of what you know but an experiment in what you think. What piece of your own thinking might shift if you wrote it down before you were sure of it? [Source](https://www.henrikkarlsson.xyz/p/writing-to-think)

*Chosen because it offers an immediately accessible reframe — writing as experiment rather than demonstration — grounded in a specific, vivid intellectual lineage.*
