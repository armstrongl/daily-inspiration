### Stone tools in a vanishing rainforest

Stone tools at a site called Bété I in Côte d'Ivoire date to 150,000 years ago, surrounded by pollen and plant waxes from dense tropical forest. Before this finding, the oldest secure evidence for humans living in African rainforests was 18,000 years ago. The site was discovered in the 1980s, its artifacts lost during the Second Ivorian Civil War, then relocated by researchers in 2020 and dated shortly before quarrying destroyed it.

[Read the source](https://www.sciencenews.org/article/humans-african-rainforests-evolution)

*Chosen for the collision of timescales: 150,000 years of human forest habitation, nearly erased by a modern civil war and a quarry, rescued in a window of months.*

### An 80-year geometry assumption disproved

For 80 years, mathematicians assumed square grids were roughly the best way to pack equal-distance pairs into a plane. An OpenAI reasoning model disproved this in May 2026 by constructing shapes in much higher dimensions using algebraic number theory, then projecting them down to 2D, where they contain polynomially more unit-distance pairs than any grid ever could. Nine mathematicians, including Tim Gowers and Noga Alon, independently verified the result.

[Read the source](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/openai-paul-erdos-geometry-problem-cracked)

*Chosen because an 80-year mathematical consensus turned out to be wrong, and the fix came from an unexpected direction: higher-dimensional algebraic structures collapsed into the plane.*

### The ambition-agency distinction

Cate Hall graduated Yale Law, clerked on the Second Circuit, and argued before the Supreme Court before recognizing she'd never actually chosen any of it. In a recent Asterisk interview, she draws a distinction most people collapse: ambition and agency aren't the same thing. You can follow a track with excellence for years without once reflecting on whether it's the track you'd pick if starting over. One question worth sitting with: pick a specific commitment you're currently running on autopilot and ask whether you'd choose it fresh.

[Read the source](https://asteriskmag.com/issues/12/can-you-just-do-things)

*Chosen because Hall's own biography makes the abstract distinction between ambition and agency viscerally concrete, and the diagnostic question requires no preparation.*
