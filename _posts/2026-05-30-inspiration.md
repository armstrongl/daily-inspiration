### The plant that modified its own enzyme

Researchers expected hornworts, the only land plants with algae-like carbon-concentrating compartments, to use the same molecular strategy as algae: a separate linker protein that gathers the enzyme Rubisco into dense clusters. Fay-Wei Li's team at the Boyce Thompson Institute found the opposite. Hornworts modified Rubisco itself, adding a short protein tail they named RbcS-STAR that works like molecular velcro. When they grafted this tail onto Arabidopsis's native Rubisco, the enzyme spontaneously clustered into compartments the plant had never built before.

[Read the source](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260311004716.htm)

*Chosen because it overturns a subfield-wide assumption: the molecular toolkit for concentrating CO2 has a fundamentally different design in land plants than in algae.*

### A gamma-ray burst that wouldn't stop

On July 2, 2025, NASA's Fermi spacecraft detected a gamma-ray burst that wouldn't stop. GRB 250702B stuttered and flared for seven hours, roughly 400 times longer than a typical burst and the longest ever recorded out of 15,000 catalogued since 1973. The light began its journey eight billion years ago. Whether a star wandered too close to a black hole and was torn apart over hours, or some engine no existing model can describe simply refused to shut off, the question is still open.

[Read the source](https://nustar.caltech.edu/news/nustar260123)

*Chosen because it is a genuine classification stress-test for astrophysics, an event that pulls existing models in two incompatible directions at once.*

### What painters leave out carries meaning

Painter Deborah Scott argues that AI didn't challenge painting at the level of recording (photography already did that). It challenged painting at the level of authorship. Her response is a framework called Structural Omission: treating what a painting leaves out not as stylistic choice but as the load-bearing architecture of the work, where gaps carry meaning. What might shift in your own creative work if you audited each omission as a structural decision rather than something you didn't get to?

*Chosen because it reframes AI's impact on creative work at a level most discussions miss, and the practice of auditing omissions transfers across domains.*
