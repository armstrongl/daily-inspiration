### Everything is the same three particles

A waterfall, a superconductor, a living cell, a conscious mind, a black hole, a tree, an iPhone. The physicist Michael Nielsen points out that every one of these is only a different arrangement of electrons, protons, and neutrons. With fine enough tweezers and enough patience, you could in principle take a person apart and rebuild them as a Bose-Einstein condensate, an iPhone, or a black hole. We keep a separate science for each of these things, yet they are all answers to one question: what can you build with three particles?

### A desert feeds a rainforest

Each year, winds lift dust from the Bodélé Depression in Chad, a dried-out ancient lake bed whose minerals are the remains of dead microorganisms, and carry it clear across the Atlantic. Using its CALIPSO satellite, NASA measured the journey in three dimensions and found that roughly 22,000 tons of phosphorus ride along annually, almost exactly the amount the Amazon loses each year to its rivers and rain. The planet's largest hot desert turns out to fertilize its largest rainforest, from thousands of miles away.

[Read the source](https://www.nasa.gov/centers-and-facilities/goddard/nasa-satellite-reveals-how-much-saharan-dust-feeds-amazons-plants/)

*Chosen because the Amazon depends on phosphorus blown in from a desert an ocean away, a connection easy to live a whole life without noticing.*

### Catch the morning light early

In a 2025 study of 1,762 adults in Brazil, every extra 30 minutes of sunlight before 10 a.m. was linked to going to sleep and waking roughly 23 minutes earlier, along with better overall sleep quality. Light later in the day helped far less. If your nights have been drifting late, you might step outside for a few minutes of morning light tomorrow and watch what happens to your sleep over the following week.

[Read the source](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12502225/)
