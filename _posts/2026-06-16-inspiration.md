### A diamond trick that broke a physics record

The same rapid-cool-under-pressure technique used to manufacture synthetic diamonds just broke a superconductivity record that stood since 1993. Physicists at the University of Houston applied "pressure quenching" to a mercury-based ceramic, pushing its transition temperature from 133 Kelvin to 151 Kelvin at normal atmospheric pressure. The material keeps its enhanced properties after the pressure is released. Borrowing a method from diamond manufacturing to solve a problem in condensed matter physics is the kind of lateral move that decades of working within the field alone had not produced.

### Meditation runs the brain's cleaning cycle awake

Your brain flushes metabolic waste during deep sleep, when cerebrospinal fluid pulses through in slow, rhythmic waves. A Vanderbilt study published in PNAS found that experienced meditators produce those same fluid dynamics while fully awake during focused attention meditation. The critical detail: a control group that matched the meditators' breathing rate showed no change in fluid flow. The cleaning required the attentional state, not just the slower breathing.

[Read the source](https://www.psypost.org/neuroscientists-find-evidence-meditation-changes-how-fluid-moves-in-the-brain/)

*Chosen because the finding that breathing rate alone is insufficient, that the attentional state is the active ingredient, is a specific and testable claim that cuts through vague meditation advice.*

### What you buy matters more than what you earn

A Cambridge study of 76,863 bank transactions found that people whose purchases matched their personality reported higher life satisfaction, and this effect outweighed both total income and total spending. Extroverts who spent more on social outings were happier; conscientious people who spent on health and fitness were happier. The researchers confirmed it experimentally by giving people vouchers for a bar or a bookshop and watching satisfaction track personality, not price. The next time a purchase feels off despite being affordable, it might be worth asking whether it fits who you are rather than what you can spend.
