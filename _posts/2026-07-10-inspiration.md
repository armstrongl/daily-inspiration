### Nine entangled particles in a crystal you can hold

Quantum entanglement is supposed to be fragile, confined to carefully isolated particles at subatomic scales. Physicists at TU Wien fired neutrons at a centimeter-sized crystal of cerium, palladium, and silicon and found that at least nine particles responded as a single entangled system, their collective sensitivity exceeding anything independent particles could produce. The crystal belongs to a class called "strange metals," and the entanglement may explain one of their puzzles: why electric current flows through them with unusually little noise.

### A dying star's light curve chirped

When supernova SN 2024afav was tracked by 27 telescopes over 200 days, its fading light didn't smooth out. It pulsed four times, each pulse closer to the last, producing what astronomers call a chirp. The explanation required general relativity: a newborn magnetar, spinning 238 times per second with a magnetic field 300 trillion times Earth's, was dragging spacetime around itself and wobbling its accretion disk into a cosmic lighthouse. It was the first direct observation of a magnetar being born inside an exploding star.

[Read the source](https://news.berkeley.edu/2026/03/11/astronomers-capture-birth-of-a-magnetar-confirming-link-to-some-of-universes-brightest-exploding-stars/)

*Chosen because four brief brightening events from a billion light-years away, decoded through general relativity, confirmed a 16-year-old theory and revealed a new kind of stellar birth in real time.*

### Your unsolved problems might benefit from a soundtrack

Northwestern researchers gave 20 people unsolved puzzles, each paired with a distinctive sound. During REM sleep, they replayed the sounds for half the puzzles. The next morning, cued puzzles were solved at twice the rate of uncued ones, and 75% of participants reported dreaming about them. One dreamer even asked a dream character for help. If you have a problem you've been stuck on, it might be worth spending a few minutes before bed deliberately thinking it through, then letting sleep do whatever it does with it.

[Read the source](https://doi.org/10.1093/nc/niaf067)

*Chosen because it turns "sleep on it" from folk wisdom into a testable claim with a specific mechanism, and the practical version requires nothing but a few minutes of focused thought before bed.*
