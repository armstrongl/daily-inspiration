### A hydrogen experiment that accidentally made graphene

David Staack's lab at Texas A&M was running a plasma reactor to split methane into hydrogen. The carbon byproduct turned out to be high-purity, single-layer graphene oxide, a nanomaterial normally manufactured from mined graphite using harsh acids. Their process, published in Nature Communications this month, uses only electricity, natural gas, and water under ambient conditions. It is the first scalable production of graphene oxide from a gas-phase precursor ever reported.

[Read the source](https://news.engineering.tamu.edu/news/2026/07/03/unexpected-discovery-yields-new-graphene-oxide-production-method/)

*Chosen because it is a clean example of how pursuing one goal can accidentally solve a harder, unrelated problem.*

### Growing rice in moon soil with a desk lamp's worth of power

A plasma device drawing less than 100 watts can pull nitrogen from the air and convert it into fertilizer. Researchers at Tohoku University and JAXA used it to grow rice seedlings in simulated lunar soil, where the fertilizer also neutralized toxic alkalinity and released trapped minerals the plants could absorb. The technology runs entirely on electricity, decoupling nitrogen fixation from both Earth's soil and fossil fuels.

[Read the source](https://www.tohoku.ac.jp/en/press/rice_grown_on_the_moon.html)

*Chosen because a 100-watt device growing rice in extraterrestrial soil is a concrete reminder that constraints can be worked around with very little.*

### Walking boosts idea generation but not idea selection

A May 2026 meta-analysis in PLOS ONE pooled 23 studies across 1,036 participants and found that walking produces a large, reliable increase in divergent thinking, the kind used for generating new ideas (effect size d = 0.93). Convergent thinking, the kind used for narrowing to a best answer, showed no change. When a problem needs fresh options rather than a final verdict, stepping away for a walk before returning to it is one of the few interventions with consistent evidence behind it.

[Read the source](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0347878)

*Chosen because the meta-analysis distinguishes between two types of thinking and shows walking helps exactly one of them, making the advice specific enough to test.*
