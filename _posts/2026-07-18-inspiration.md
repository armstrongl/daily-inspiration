### The failed argument that became a photonics tool

In 1818, mathematician Siméon-Denis Poisson argued that Augustin Fresnel's wave theory of light predicted an absurdity: a bright spot at the exact center of a circular object's shadow. François Arago ran the experiment and the spot appeared, vindicating wave theory. Two hundred years later, physicist Shen Yijie at NTU Singapore aimed a laser at a small disc and found that the same Poisson spot generates four types of optical skyrmions simultaneously, exotic topological structures in light that normally require expensive metamaterials to produce.

### Five thousand creatures, one streetlight

On summer nights in the Golan Heights, amateur naturalist Eviatar Itzkovich noticed thousands of pill bugs abandoning their solitary lives under stones to form synchronized circular processions beneath streetlights, some mills exceeding 5,000 individuals. Researchers at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem confirmed the phenomenon had never been documented in any isopod species. The creatures track the circular boundary where light meets dark on the ground, and once enough individuals join, the movement becomes self-sustaining, a collective behavior emerging from a geometry that didn't exist before we put lights on poles.

*Chosen because the scale of collective behavior produced by something as mundane as a streetlight exceeds what most people imagine is happening at their feet after dark.*

### A new genus found by looking carefully

A citizen scientist exploring remote terrain in Big Bend National Park photographed an unfamiliar plant and uploaded the image to iNaturalist. Expert botanists who reviewed it recognized something none of them had described before: Ovicula biradiata, not just a new species but an entirely new genus in the daisy family. A 2026 study in the American Journal of Botany found at least 12 new plant species discovered this way, each one spotted by someone who looked carefully and shared what they saw. What's growing, unrecognized, in the places you already walk through?

[Read the source](https://bsapubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ajb2.70048)

*Chosen because it names a reachable practice, photographing and uploading plants, that has already produced discoveries at the genus level, making the invitation concrete rather than aspirational.*
