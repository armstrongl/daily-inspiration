### The Alzheimer's protein that builds lasting memory

For decades, tau has been cast as the destructive agent in Alzheimer's disease, the protein whose tangles track most closely with memory loss. A Flinders University team reported in Nature Communications that healthy tau is actually required for encoding long-term memories. Mice without functional tau learned normally and recalled events hours later but could not retrieve them weeks afterward. When the researchers used light to directly activate the original memory-storing neurons, the memories came back, proving they were physically intact but unreachable without tau's help.

[Read the source](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-73207-9)

*Chosen because the reversal, the molecule most blamed for destroying memory is required for building it, forces a concrete reassessment of ongoing drug development strategies.*

### Sixty-two lynx, then 2,663

In 2002, fewer than 100 Iberian lynx survived on Earth, making it the most endangered cat species. The 2025 census counted 2,663 across Spain and Portugal, with 542 breeding females and 952 cubs born in a single year. Twenty-four years of habitat restoration, captive breeding, and rabbit population management produced the largest cat species recovery ever documented.

[Read the source](https://fundacion-biodiversidad.es/en/notas_de_prensa/the-iberian-lynx-population-reached-2663-specimens-in-2025/)

*Chosen because the recovery scale, from 62 individuals to 2,663 with 952 cubs in one year, is specific enough to resist the vagueness of feel-good conservation narratives.*

### Defenses built before the threat arrives

The chytrid fungus has driven more amphibian species to extinction than any other infectious disease. In the Pyrenees, it nearly wiped out common midwife toads at four mountain lakes, but at three of those lakes the populations recovered. A study in Nature Chemical Biology found the survivors had begun secreting antimicrobial peptides while still tadpoles, a life stage the fungus cannot even attack, building their chemical arsenal during the window when nothing yet threatened them. What's one capacity you've been meaning to develop while conditions still allow for unhurried practice?
