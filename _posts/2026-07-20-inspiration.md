### Eighty years of geometric consensus, broken

Paul Erdős asked in 1946 how many pairs among n points in the plane can be exactly one unit apart. For 80 years, the square grid appeared to be essentially optimal, and most mathematicians believed no arrangement could do meaningfully better. A May 2026 construction using algebraic number fields broke that ceiling by a fixed polynomial margin, verified and published by nine mathematicians including Noga Alon and Timothy Gowers.

### A houseplant doing advanced geometry

The Chinese money plant, one of the world's most common houseplants, builds its leaf veins in Voronoi diagrams. Water-secreting pores called hydathodes serve as center points, and the hormone auxin spreads outward from each pore until neighboring waves collide and harden into vein walls, forming the diagram's edges. Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory researchers reported in Nature Communications that this is the first case where both the centers and boundaries of a naturally occurring Voronoi diagram are visible and functional in any plant's venation.

[Read the source](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-71768-3)

*Chosen because the discovery that a common houseplant performs computational geometry in its leaf veins makes a familiar object feel newly interesting.*

### Your brain underestimates this learning strategy

A 2026 study in the British Journal of Educational Psychology found that college students who deliberately generated wrong answers before seeing correct ones transferred knowledge to new domains more effectively than students who used retrieval practice, the current gold standard. The students themselves rated deliberate erring as the weakest strategy, even as their transfer scores showed it was the strongest. What's one thing you're currently learning where you might try generating a wrong answer first, just to see what your mistake reveals?

[Read the source](https://bpspsychub.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/bjep.70058)

*Chosen because the mismatch between what feels effective and what measurably transfers is specific enough to change how you practice tomorrow.*
